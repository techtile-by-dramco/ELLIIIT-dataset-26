\section{Conclusion}

This paper documented \dataset{} as a synchronized measurement campaign and data release rather than as a single benchmark score sheet. The campaign captures a synchronized workflow for rover motion, Qualisys positioning, acoustic acquisition, and distributed ceiling-tile \gls{rf} reciprocity measurements in Techtile. In the current release state, that workflow yields a 12-experiment campaign with 7{,}579 logged rover stops, a merged 9-experiment \gls{rf} dataset with 5{,}011 populated \gls{rf} cycles and 210{,}068 complex \gls{csi} values, and a fully aligned tri-modal subset of 2{,}872 cycle pairs.

The central result of this paper is therefore clarity: \dataset{} already supports campaign-level spatial analysis, tile-level \gls{rf} interrogation, and cycle-level \gls{rf}+acoustic+position joining, but those capabilities depend on choosing the correct completeness subset. The \gls{rf} modality provides distributed complex channel fingerprints, the acoustic modality provides synchronized waveform responses, and the shared ground-truth positions make both modalities directly usable for RF-only, acoustic-only, or fused positioning methods. By making the experiment coverage, recorded products, and interpretation constraints explicit, the article turns \dataset{} into a more reliable experimental reference for future distributed communication and multimodal sensing research.

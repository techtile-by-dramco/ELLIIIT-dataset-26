\begin{abstract}
This paper documents \dataset{}, a synchronized measurement campaign for distributed wireless sensing and positioning in the Techtile testbed. The acquisition stack coordinates rover motion, Qualisys-based ground truth, acoustic chirp capture, and radio-frequency (\gls{rf}) reciprocity measurements. Each acquisition cycle corresponds to one rover stop, one position sample, one acoustic recording, and one \gls{rf} snapshot across the active ceiling aperture. The \gls{rf} modality is recorded as per-host pilot measurements and released as complex channel-state-information tensors, while the acoustic modality is recorded as synchronized microphone waveforms and released as aligned waveform tensors. The campaign spans 12 named experiments and 7{,}579 rover position samples. The merged \gls{rf} NetCDF exposes 9 experiments, 5{,}011 populated \gls{rf} cycles, 42 ceiling receiver tiles, and 210{,}068 complex channel-state-information samples. A corrected completeness analysis identifies 2{,}096 currently readable acoustic cycles and 2{,}095 fully aligned \gls{rf}+rover+acoustic cycle pairs spanning 5.57~m in $x$ and 2.60~m in $y$. Beyond documenting the recorded assets, we also report the now-implemented downstream experiments: a walk-based acoustic positioning pipeline that performs anchor selection, pulse-compression ranging, and least-squares localization on held-out rover trajectories, together with exploratory phase-based \gls{rf} positioning utilities. We detail how the measurements are recorded, quantify per-experiment coverage, describe the RF and acoustic data contents, and explain how the aligned modalities support RF-only, acoustic-only, and joint positioning workflows.
\end{abstract}
